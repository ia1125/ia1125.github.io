CUTAI is not a usual tea. Crafted with suprise in mind, it explores the creation of a tasteful product, even from unsuccessful harvests.

A message from the connoisseur

This first project started with a simple prompt; make a video in 30 minutes. My group and I ran around our university campus trying to shoot some videos in hope to combine them into a meaningless, internet-worthy clip. While that felt entertaining, the prompt unfolded for a second mission seemingly impossible for me to achieve. Laconically, make a website to host your video.

I immediately thought that I will not follow a paved road. Making a website for only one video, that also barely carried meaning, did not seem as a constructive dedication of my time.

On a weekend morning, I come up with an idea. How about making it all about a product? Something that is useful. Something that solves a problem. As someone who edits a lot of videos for work, I knew the struggle of media management and other technical difficulties that interrupt the workflow. So, what if, my product was an AI-powered software that made all the editing for me? This would resolve the problem with our 30 minute video; rather than finding ideas on how to recycle that footage, I will let my program do the work.

With this idea, I started looking for a title. I have to admit that the outcome isn't original; CUTAI simply brings together "cut" (like in movies) and "AI". It's like starting a brand called KettleWarmer...

A few words about the process

It all started with a wireframe. I have exported most of my graphics as JPGs or PNGs and overlayed them on top of each other (as opposed to doing it all by code).

The contrasted colors refer to the two key elements of the project; the video editing aspect (orange color), and the role of AI (purple/blue color).

The overall color scheme of the website is dark in reference to professional video editing tools (such as Premiere Pro).
